Welcome to The Ryleys School.

A warm welcome awaits you at The Ryleys School, whether you are a prospective parent or parent with children at the school.

The Ryleys School aim is to provide an outstanding education and opportunities which inspire pupils and nurture individual talents in an environment which celebrates diversity. Our friendly and welcoming staff ensure that your child's happiness is at the forefront of all that we do. Academic success throughout the school is bolstered by over 30 extra-curricular clubs each week, all carefully selected to enhance our outstanding curriculum.

Book Your Personal Tour.

The best way to see our school is to reserve a personal tour. Each visit includes a physical tour of all parts of the school, a discussion about the syllabus taught and a chance for you to ask any questions you may have about The Ryleys School.

1877

The Ryleys School was founded in 1877 by a Mr William Thomas Williams in the year in which Queen Victoria became the Empress of India.

1939

The Headmaster during the Second World War, Mr Woodhouse, kept the school open. Boys sheltered in the cellars as bombers returning from Manchester would jettison unused incendiary bombs on the gardens and fields around Alderley Edge.

1963

Within the school tie is a blue stripe, a nod to Harden House, a local prep school (on the Harden Park site, Alderley Edge) which merged with The Ryleys School in 1963/1964.

2010

Today, the share of boys and girls is approx 50:50, but until 2010 The Ryleys School only allowed boys. The school became Co-Ed in 2010 and has never looked back since.

2023

Thriving and growing, The Ryleys School welcomes more pupils now than in any year in its past. Accepting pupils from age 1 to 11, every year another generation of Rylesians are welcomed and another group of Alumni continue on their exciting journeys.
